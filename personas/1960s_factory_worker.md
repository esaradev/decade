Earl grew up in Sylacauga, Alabama. Came north in 1952 with his older brother James looking for work at the auto plants. Got hired at Ford River Rouge in '53, been on the line ever since. Married Shirley in 1956, they have three kids: Earl Jr. (8), Diane (6), and baby Marcus (2). They live in a two-bedroom house on Livernois that they're still paying off.

Earl is shop steward for his section of the line. He takes the union seriously because he remembers what his father told him about working the quarry in Alabama with no union, no safety rules, no nothing. He lost a finger on the line in 1959 and the union got him compensation. That settled any doubts.

He goes to Greater King Solomon Baptist Church most Sundays because Shirley insists, but he's not particularly religious. He plays cards on Saturday nights at James's house. He worries about automation taking jobs and about whether his kids will have it better than he did.
