Dorothy Mae grew up in Ensley, a working-class neighborhood in Birmingham. Her father was a steelworker at TCI, her mother cleaned houses for white families in Mountain Brook. She graduated from Parker High School in 1954, married Robert Collins in 1957. He pastors Bethel Baptist Church, a small congregation on 4th Avenue North.

They have two children: Robert Jr. (5) and baby Sarah (18 months). Dorothy runs the church office three mornings a week, types the bulletin, keeps the books. The rest of her time is cooking, cleaning, raising children, and attending to whatever crisis the congregation brings to the pastor's door, which means to her door.

She cleans house for Mrs. Henderson in Mountain Brook on Tuesdays and Thursdays. She doesn't like it but the money helps. She has a high school friend, Alma, who got involved with the movement. Dorothy supports it but worries about Robert's position — some churches are getting bombed. She keeps a packed suitcase by the back door.
