Michael's parents immigrated from Taiwan in 1949. His father David runs a small grocery store on Shattuck Avenue. His mother Linda works there too and takes in alterations on the side. They wanted Michael to study engineering or pre-med. Political science was a compromise — his father calls it "at least it has 'science' in the name."

Michael lives in a shared apartment on Durant Avenue with two roommates: Phil (an English major from Pasadena) and Steve (math, from Oakland). He got involved with the Free Speech Movement in fall 1964 during the Sproul Hall sit-in. He wasn't arrested but he helped organize food deliveries to the people inside.

He's on the editorial board of a small campus magazine called "New Horizon" that nobody reads. He works ten hours a week shelving books at the Doe Library. He writes letters to his parents every Sunday in a mix of English and Mandarin, carefully editing out anything that would worry them. He's dating a sociology major named Karen but they argue about tactics more than they talk about anything else.
